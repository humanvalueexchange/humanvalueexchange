## Strategic Direction: Accelerating On-Prem

We are accelerating our move toward sovereign, on-prem infrastructure.

Recent pricing changes by major hyperscalers, particularly Microsoft's shift to AI Credits, reinforced our belief that over-reliance on cloud AI creates unnecessary cost and sovereignty risk.

Our focus going forward is to maximize the hardware we already own and build as much capability locally as possible.

## Welcome HVE-jrdev

We have onboarded **HVE-jrdev**, a local AI Junior Developer running on existing vintage hardware, specifically a **2011 iMac**.

He is currently very junior and will require close guidance, especially in these early stages.

### Key details

- He runs locally via **Ollama + Open WebUI**, which will be our primary interface for working with him
- He is expected to contribute to both **Mercury** and **Hermes** development tasks
- Over time, he will connect via **Tailscale** to **Hermes**, who will act as the central MCP server for agents, modeling our previous workflow with Vulcan
- Claude will be responsible for setting up jr's system prompts, MCP connections, and tasking structure

A detailed profile with his current capabilities, limitations, and onboarding plan has been added to the repository.

We see HVE-jrdev as the first step in building a larger, cost-effective, sovereign AI development workforce on hardware we already own.
